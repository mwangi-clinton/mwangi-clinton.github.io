About Me

I am a Master's student in Telecommunication Engineering with a strong passion for harnessing the power of Artificial Intelligence to revolutionize healthcare. Currently, I am a research intern at the Center for Data Science and AI (DsaIL) at Dedan Kimathi University of Technology, where I am focused on the intersection of AI and medical technologies. My academic journey has equipped me with a deep understanding of telecommunications, while my research efforts allow me to explore innovative applications of AI to improve patient outcomes.

At DsaIL, I am developing advanced solutions aimed at assisting orthopedic surgeons in diagnosing lower limb deformities. By leveraging cutting-edge technologies such as computer vision, machine learning, and real-time data processing, I work on projects that aim to provide more accurate diagnoses and improve treatment plans. My focus is on creating tools that enhance the clinical decision-making process and support surgeons in delivering precise and efficient care.

Beyond my research, I am driven by the potential of AI to bring about positive change in healthcare, particularly in areas like orthopedics, where early detection and proper treatment are crucial for long-term recovery. I am constantly exploring new ways to combine my technical expertise with my passion for improving patient care, and I am excited to contribute to innovations that will make a real difference in the lives of individuals recovering from injury and illness.
